## 1. Introduction

**What is the problem?**
The problem involves classifying medical X-ray images into one of the 20 distinct categories using deep learning techniques. 

**Why is it important?**
Automating the classification of X-ray images can significantly speed up medical diagnoses, reduce the workload on healthcare professionals, and minimize human error, leading to more efficient, accurate, and scalable patient care.

**What is the dataset?**
The dataset provided is from the Kaggle competition `26-t-1-dl-gen-ainppe-1`, comprising a training set of 51,043 X-ray images and a test set of 17,015 images spread across 20 distinct classes.

**What is your objective?**
The primary objective is to build and train a robust Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) capable of accurately identifying and classifying these X-ray images into their target categories, optimizing for a custom penalty-based evaluation metric.

## 2. Methodology

**What models you used:**
I utilized a ResNet18 (Residual Network with 18 layers) architecture.

**Why you chose them:**
ResNet18 was chosen because it represents a good balance of computational efficiency and accuracy. Its residual connections fundamentally help prevent vanishing gradients when training deeper networks, making it highly effective for image classification baselines. Given resource and runtime constraints, ResNet18 trains significantly faster than heavier architectures (like ResNet50) while still capturing complex visual features effectively.

**Preprocessing steps:**
- **Resizing:** Images were resized from their raw dimensions to 224x224 pixels to match the input specifications expected by the ResNet architecture.
- **Normalization:** Standard ImageNet normalization was applied using the established mean `[0.485, 0.456, 0.406]` and standard deviation `[0.229, 0.224, 0.225]` to align the data distribution with the pre-trained weights.

**Any techniques:**
- **Data Augmentation:** Used Random Horizontal Flip (`p=0.5`) during training to increase the diversity of the training data and reduce overfitting.
- **Transfer Learning:** Initialized the model with pre-trained ImageNet weights (`ResNet18_Weights.DEFAULT`). The final fully connected layer (`model.fc`) was modified to output 20 classes instead of 1000.
- **Handling Imbalance:** Handled dataset class imbalance directly by computing proportional class weights and passing them into the `CrossEntropyLoss` function.
- **Optimization:** Utilized the `AdamW` optimizer for better weight decay properties compared to standard Adam, alongside mixed precision training (`torch.cuda.amp.autocast`) to accelerate execution on the GPU.

## 3. Experiments and Results

**What experiments you performed:**
To keep runtime under control and allow for faster iterative testing, a smaller stratified subset of 25,000 images was used for experimentation. The model was trained aggressively utilizing mixed precision on a Tesla T4 GPU.

**What variations you tried:**
- **Validation strategy:** Performed an 85/15 train/validation split on the 25,000-image subset using a stratified split (based on target labels) to ensure identical distribution across both training and validation sets.
- **Hyperparameter configurations:** Explored a robust learning rate of `1e-3`, an aggressive batch size of `64` to maximize batch normalization effects, tracking across 5 fully recorded epochs.
